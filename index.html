Rhetorical Situation

The Context of the Album
Background on the Writers / Musicians
Who is Sade? The band's formation, members (Sade Adu, Stuart Matthewman, Paul Denman, Andrew Hale), their musical journey, and the four-year hiatus before Love Deluxe.
The lead vocalist is Sade Adu is a British-Nigerian R&B/Jazz artist who was born in Nigeria but moved to Britain at 4 years old. Before making music, she studied at St. Martins School of Art and worked as a model as well as a fashion designer. Stuart Matthewman was a songwriter as well as the Guitarist who helped co-found Sade. Paul Denman was an English bassist who helped create bass lines for all of Sade's music. Andrew Hale was a keyboardist who was responsible for the bands mellow production style, and he later went on to create music for movies and television.
Genre & Tone
R&B, ambient, cool jazz, sophisti-pop, quiet storm. The shift to programmed, lush production. The album's mood, atmosphere, and sonic palette.
Sade's music falls into the R&B and jazz genres. The music Sade makes is known for its atmospheric and smooth feel. The band placed a focus on using soft vocals and instrumentation to create a sense of intimacy. The album is described as being minimalist, with the cover photo even being in black and white. Sade's chosen methods set their music apart from the majority of artists in the R&B scene.
What Is Happening in the World Leading Up to Its Release
1992 — post-Cold War, LA Riots, Clinton vs. Bush, rise of hip-hop, grunge explosion, the Somali Civil War, and the broader cultural moment.
Sade was influenced by the cultural changes in the early 1990s. Hip-Hop and alternative rock music was becoming popular. More and more artists were beginning to experiment with emotional music. This all was because the 1990s was a period of immense racial tension and social unrest. Grunge music was beginning to become popular at the time which signified a shift to more emotional music. Though a majority of this emotional music was loud and aggressive, which was the opposite of what Sade brought to the genre. The early 1990s saw the end of the Cold War, the Persian Gulf War, and a global economic recession.
Specific Information That Prompted & Impacted This Product
What personal, artistic, or industry events shaped Love Deluxe? The decision to modernize their sound, recording across Venice, London, Surrey, and Los Angeles.
Sade wanted to create a more mature and sophisticated type of music that still conveyed a message that average people could relate to. The band also released "Love Deluxe" after taking a four year break which contributed to the album's reflective tone. While Sade's music is universally soft and slower paced, "Love Deluxe" sought to create an even more emotionally immersive sound.
Is There a Core Message
The unifying themes — love as complex, painful, transformative. Desire and heartache. Vulnerability beneath a composed surface.
The core message behind Love Deluxe is that Love can heal but also hurt. The album has songs that many would consider to be sad, and songs that many would consider to be happy. The varying emotions are done to convey the feelings that come with heartbreak and recovery.
By Love Deluxe, the understatement had attained Zen.
Chris Roberts — Uncut

Lyrical Analysis

Deep Dive — Two Songs

Choose two songs — consider songs that are connected, on opposite ends of the album, or that have different tones. For each: analyze meaning over verses, discuss the chorus, define allusions, identify at least two literary elements, and explain the song's contribution to the album.

Song One
Like a Tattoo
Meaning Development Over Verses
The song reveals a soldier's regrets as he gets closer to his death. The man in the song has killed another man and cannot wash away the guilt before he himself is killed. As the song progresses his memory gets clearer and clearer, and in turn he feels more and more regret. By the end of the song, the listener has reached a state of understanding as to how deeply the soldier's guilt has affected him.
Chorus Analysis
The "Like a tattoo" title and chorus symbolises the permanent emotional scars that come with trauma. It conveys that memories cannot be removed and they become a part of your character.
Allusions
The song is referencing war and violence. It is able to convey the feelings that arise from conflict by speaking about a soldier's traumatic end.
Literary Elements (At Least Two)
The song uses vivid storytelling and visual descriptions to paint an image in the listeners mind about what this soldier is going through before his death. The tattoo is symbolic of this guilt that the soldier will never be able to wash away. The tone is very reflective and mournful. Sade also uses imagery to make the story feel personal, as the listener can envision what's going on around the soldier and what's going on in his head.
Contribution to the Album
This is one of the darkest and most emotional songs on the album. It expands the album beyond the suffering that comes from romance and expands into human suffering.
Song Two
Kiss of Life
Meaning Development Over Verses
Kiss of life is a song that begins with emotional emptiness and slowly becomes happier through being fulfilled by love. The speaker is brought back to life by a long lasting connection with someone else. The warm sound of the song makes the listener feel more and more healed as the song progresses.
Chorus Analysis
The kiss of life is a metaphor for revival. The speaker is emotionally revived by love and as a result feels much happier and shifts the song to a hopeful mood.
Allusions
The song references revival through the kiss of life. It alludes to being brought back to life emotionally.
Literary Elements (At Least Two)
Love is compared to revival which is a metaphor for the power of love. The tone shifts from loneliness to warmth as the speaker is revived. The song also uses warm imagery to create a comforting atmosphere in the listener's mind. The repetition throughout the chorus emphasises the importance of emotional connection in life, and how it can brighten everything around you.
Contribution to the Album
The song shows that you can be healed through love and balances out the other sad tracks. It brings warmth to the album and optimism to the album.
An artfully arranged and tastefully executed album that repays the time it takes to grow on you.
Mark Coleman — Rolling Stone

Intentional Choices in Order
The album starts with intense feelings of heartbreak and vulnerability. But as the album progresses it becomes more reflective and atmospheric and focuses on healing. Songs at the beginning focus on emotional pain, but later songs focus on feelings of introspection and being calm. These deliberate choices cause the album to feel like an emotional journey.
Tone Shifts & Purpose
Songs like "Like a tattoo" are very serious and dark. But later songs like "Kiss of life" are very hopeful and inspiring. The contrast between sadness and hope reinforces the album's message that love can hurt but also heal people emotionally.
How Meaning Is Developed Wholly
The album comes together to explore the highs and lows of love. It shows how it can be painful but incredibly fulfilling.

Album Impact & Legacy

Then, Now & Why It Matters
Love Deluxe is an exercise in pure immersion; the air and textures so thick and inky, the mere act of listening to it feels like slowly swimming through a body of water.
Apple Music — No. 61 on 100 Best Albums
Reception at the Time & Now
The album's reception has been largely the same all throughout history. It has always been praised for its depth and smooth feel. Critics praised the album for its emotional maturity and atmospheric vibe. It frequently is considered to be Sade's best album and often appears on "greatest album" lists.
Impact on Music & Genre
"Love Deluxe" influenced modern R&B artists to use minimalist production in their music. Sade also helped to popularize atmospheric R&B styles. Examples of similar artists are Frank Ocean, The Weeknd, and Jessie Ware. They all have created songs with similar feelings to Sade's music which shows Sade's music continues to impact music to this day.
Why This Album Is Important to You
I discovered the song "Kiss of Life" 5 years ago through a racing video games soundtrack. I then went on to listen to the rest of Sade's music and have enjoyed it ever since. So Sade's music has a nostalgic feel to it.
